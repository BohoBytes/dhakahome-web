FAQ's

Can a home depreciate in value?
Yes, homes can depreciate in value due to various factors such as market conditions, property condition, location changes, or economic downturns. Regular maintenance and strategic improvements can help maintain or increase property value.
Is an older home as good a value as a new home?
Both older and new homes have their advantages. Older homes often feature unique character, established neighborhoods, and mature landscaping. New homes typically offer modern amenities, energy efficiency, and lower maintenance costs. The best value depends on your priorities and budget.
Can I pay my own taxes and insurance?
Yes, you can typically pay your property taxes and insurance directly. However, many lenders require an escrow account where monthly payments are collected and used to pay these expenses on your behalf. This ensures timely payments and protects the lender's investment.
How long does the loan process take?
The loan process typically takes 30-45 days from application to closing. This timeline can vary based on factors such as loan type, documentation completeness, property appraisal, and lender workload. Pre-approval and having all documents ready can help expedite the process.
